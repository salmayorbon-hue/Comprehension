    <h2>Etymology</h2>
    <p>
       From Latin comprehendere, meaning “to grasp,” “to seize,” or “to understand.” The idea is similar to mentally “grasping” information.

    </p>

    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        Comprehension is the ability to understand, interpret, and make sense of information.

    </p>

    <h3>Denotation</h3>
    <p>
        The denotation is the literal, objective meaning:
    <ul>
        <li>Understanding something clearly and correctly.
</li>
        <li</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="example">
        <strong>Example (Denotation):</strong><br>
       Good reading habits improve comprehension skills over time.
    </div>

    <h3>Connotation</h3>
    <p>
      The word has a positive meaning. It suggests intelligence, clarity, and mental ability.
